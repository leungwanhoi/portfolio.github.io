My Projects

Forecasting and Data Analysis on Climate Change in Hong Kong

This project focuses on HK climate situation about different temperature measures as well as mean sea levels. It aims to visualize the past and the recent climate measures in HK, and then forecast the future climate measures in HK.

Titanic Survival Prediction with Machine Learning

This project focuses on making predictions of the titanic iceberg accident survival using classification. Here me and my groupmates implemented Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest and Linear Regression make comparison of the prediction accuracy.

  • More

Analysis of the Growth of plants watered by different kinds of solutions

The purpose of the experiment is to discover the conditions that favour the growth of the green beans most. 3 conditions are chosen in this experiment, which are distilled water, mineral water and tap water. After 2 weeks, we collect data to perform data visualzation, input 95% confidence interval and perform ANOVA analysis using SAS.

Prediction of Vehicle Carbon Dioxide Emissions by Statistical Learning Models

This project is to shortlist a machine learning algorithm with the greatest predictive power of carbon dioxide emissions based on critical features, such that consumers can compare and purchase vehicles with longer lifetime and lower maintenance costs, saving money in the long run.

A Hidden Markov Model for Hong Kong Rainfall Data

This project is to make predictions on the hidden state of HK rainfall data from 1947 to 2021 by utilizing decoding problems and to find out the optimal sequence by using Algorithms, and also to make predictions on probability by utilizing the Hidden Markov Model(HMM) Algorithm.

  • More

Business Analytics of Successful Movies

This study aim to analyze the factors that contribute to the success of movies using data cleaning techniques in R. After cleaning the data, descriptive statistics and exploratory data analysis techniques were used to identify patterns and relationships between the variables.
